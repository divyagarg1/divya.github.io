Overview

The Strategic Highway Safety Plan (SHSP) is a statewide, coordinated safety plan that provides a comprehensive framework for reducing highway fatalities and severe injuries on all public roads. It identifies key safety needs in terms of challenge areas and each Challenge Area Team selects the actions to implement each of the strategies that are part of their implementation plan in order to improve public safety.

As a Graduate Student Researcher for SafeTREC, Berkeley, I was responsible for the new design and development of SHSP tracking website used by the challenge area and action leaders to update information related to the status of actions and their associated output and outcome performance measures. I not only designed a user-friendly website but also recommended and developed new features like comprehensive querying interface to generate intuitive vizualization reports and an interactive dashboard with rich charts and graphs so that agency stakeholders can observe progress and identify obstacles in real time.

Research

We outlined and conducted surveys and focus group interviews for the steering committee members and agency stakeholders before and after the beta release that helped in planning and prioritizing feature changes. We also conducted webinar and usability testing for challenge area leaders and action leaders to validate our design and to find usability issues before the launch date.
